THE LAMB AND THE HOMELESS MAN

I wanted to read this book because I know Rusty often preaches and ministers to the homeless people that he once knew. He is a true success story of someone coming out of homelessness and being radically changed by Jesus Christ.

I appreciate his brutal honesty about his addiction and his life spiraling out of control. He didn’t hold back. He bared it all. This doesn’t leave the reader, or the would-be recovering addict, or someone facing homelessness, guessing how bad Rusty’s homeless life really was.

Rusty tells in gruesome detail the tactics he resorted to survive and to support his habits. Lying and stealing were a way of life. Rusty peels back the layers and reveals faithfully what that old life was like.

One of my favorite parts of the book is the supernatural aspect of his conversion. He illustrates his experience with the demonic realm and moves into the spiritual relationship with Jesus. He also provides ample scriptures so you can follow along with how God was illuminating his walk along The Way.

This is a page turner, that you will impact you greatly.

The Lamb and The Homeless Man was a page turner from the start! As one who has experienced the grace of God in the midst of impossible odds, I am always interested in the stories of others. This book gives an authentic look into the areas of family abuse, alcohol and drug addiction, homelessness, and criminal manipulation. It is all the more gripping as it is told from the perspective of personal experience. I was amazed, amused, and greatly encouraged at how the Hand of Christ could be seen at work; sometimes behind the scenes, and at other times in mind-blowing ways. This is definitely a book that will be a permanent part of my personal library.

Cloverdale Baptist Church
Grand Bay, AL  

"The Lamb and the Homeless Man" is an eye opening look into the life of one single homeless person. As I read Rusty's personal story of 5 years of living on the streets, I felt as if I was traveling with him. Reading this interesting book will certainly help you understand not just the needs of the homeless community, but you will learn how they think and live day by day, month after month, year after year. I have always had the thought when I see a homeless person that I am looking at someone's father, mother, brother, sister, son,or husband. Now I know that it's true, because Rusty is a father, a son, a brother, and a husband. This book tells his story of rescue and restoration, through the eyes and the testimony of the homeless man, and you'll never view the homeless person the same after reading this book.

Terry Ragsdale
